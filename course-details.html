Topics

The 8th class math curriculum typically covers foundational topics that help students build a strong base in mathematics. 1. Rational Numbers Properties of rational numbers (closure, commutative, associative, distributive). Representation of rational numbers on the number line. Operations on rational numbers (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division). Finding rational numbers between two rational numbers. 2. Linear Equations in One Variable Introduction to linear equations. Solving equations with variables on one side and both sides. Applications of linear equations in solving real-life problems. 3. Understanding Quadrilaterals Types of quadrilaterals (parallelogram, rectangle, square, rhombus, trapezium). Properties of quadrilaterals. Angle sum property of quadrilaterals. 4. Practical Geometry Construction of quadrilaterals when different parameters are given (e.g., sides and angles). Construction of special types of quadrilaterals using a compass and straightedge. 5. Data Handling Organizing and representing data. Graphical representation: bar graphs, histograms, pie charts. Probability: Introduction and simple problems. Mean, median, and mode of data sets. 6. Squares and Square Roots Properties of square numbers. Finding squares of numbers. Finding square roots using the prime factorization method and long division method. Estimating square roots. 7.etc.. These topics are designed to strengthen students' understanding of basic mathematical concepts and prepare them for more advanced studies in the following years.
